# 006 — Knowledge Base

Version: 1.0

Status: Approved

Document Type: Architecture Specification

---

# Purpose

The Knowledge Base is the permanent memory of the Trading Research Platform (TRP).

Its purpose is to preserve, organize, connect, and continuously expand every piece of valuable knowledge produced by the platform.

Unlike traditional trading systems that only store historical results, TRP builds an ever-growing body of reusable knowledge.

Knowledge is the most valuable asset of the platform.

---

# Philosophy

Research without memory repeats mistakes.

Knowledge transforms isolated experiments into cumulative intelligence.

Every experiment should leave the platform smarter than before.

Nothing valuable should ever disappear.

---

# Mission

The mission of the Knowledge Base is to convert:

- Experiments
- Validation
- Production
- Human expertise
- AI discoveries

into reusable knowledge.

---

# Core Principle

The platform never asks:

> What happened?

Instead it asks:

> What have we learned?

# Types of Knowledge

The Knowledge Base stores multiple categories.

---

## Research Knowledge

Generated during experiments.

Examples:

- Successful parameter ranges
- Failed parameter ranges
- Strategy limitations
- Market behavior
- Unexpected observations

---

## Validation Knowledge

Generated during scientific verification.

Examples:

- Robustness
- Stability
- Weaknesses
- Failure conditions
- Confidence levels

---

## Production Knowledge

Generated during live trading.

Examples:

- Real drawdowns
- Execution latency
- Slippage behavior
- Exchange reliability
- Long-term stability

---

## AI Knowledge

Generated by AI analysis.

Examples:

- Hidden patterns
- Similar strategies
- Risk observations
- Research summaries
- Improvement suggestions

---

## Human Knowledge

Generated by researchers.

Examples:

- Notes
- Decisions
- Conclusions
- Ideas
- Lessons learned

---

# Knowledge Item

Knowledge is stored as individual Knowledge Items.

Each item represents one reusable fact.

Examples:

- ATR values above 35 reduce stability.
- BTC momentum strategies fail during low volatility.
- Binance slippage increases significantly during funding events.
- Strategy Version 2 performs better during bull markets.

Knowledge Items are atomic.

One fact.

One observation.

One conclusion.

---

# Knowledge Categories

Knowledge Items are classified.

Examples:

- Indicators
- Parameters
- Validation
- Production

Classification improves discovery.

# Search

The Knowledge Base provides intelligent search.

Researchers may search by:

- Strategy
- Indicator
- Exchange
- Instrument
- Risk
- Metric
- AI Observation
- Experiment
- Validation
- Report

Search should return knowledge—not files.

---

# Similarity Search

Researchers may ask:

- Show similar experiments.
- Show similar failures.
- Find strategies with comparable behavior.
- Find experiments using similar parameters.

Similarity accelerates research.

# Experiment History

Every experiment remains permanently accessible.

Researchers may review:

- Configuration
- Metrics
- Reports
- AI observations
- Human notes
- Validation

History is never deleted.

---

# AI Integration

The AI System uses the Knowledge Base as its primary source of context.

AI may:

- Explain previous experiments
- Find similar research
- Generate summaries
- Detect recurring failures
- Recommend future work

AI should answer using accumulated knowledge rather than isolated reports.

---

# Recommendation Engine

Recommendations are generated using accumulated knowledge.

Examples:

- Repeat this experiment.
- Expand the dataset.
- Avoid these parameter ranges.
- Compare with Strategy Version 4.
- Test another market.

Recommendations are evidence-based.

# Knowledge Quality

Knowledge Items should be:

- Traceable
- Explainable
- Reproducible
- Searchable
- Linked
- Permanent

Unverified assumptions are never stored as facts.

# AI Contributions

AI may generate:

# Versioning

Knowledge evolves.

Instead:

```

↓

Revision

↓

↓

Revision
```

The evolution remains visible.

# Long-Term Vision

After years of operation the Knowledge Base becomes one of the platform's greatest assets.

Instead of remembering thousands of experiments, researchers explore accumulated intelligence.

The system becomes increasingly valuable as knowledge grows.

---

# Success Criteria

A successful Knowledge Base:

- Never loses valuable information.
- Connects related knowledge.
- Supports AI reasoning.
- Accelerates research.
- Prevents repeated mistakes.
- Improves every future experiment.
